What happens when a small team of information designers and computer scientists decide to change the way that the genomic and proteomic data is currently visualized?

We found a problem


+ 198 000 gene transcriptions are currently stored in the Ensembl Database

There is so much data that is currently not visualized properly.



... and therefore an opportunity

About the data


We extracted genomic and proteomic data from Ensembl.org and analized how to relate the information with pharmacogenomic data contained in PharmGKB database

Getting involved in the project


Then we tried to understand the user and analyzed the data, we also had brainstorming and sketch sessions in order to come up with intuitive and interactive data visualizations

Coming up with a Solution


Finally we developed a tool that has a better data visualization of trustable sources, which allows the researcher to quickly understand the information about proteins related to certain diseases

Our tool proposes a new way of visualizing proteomic and genomic data, by redesigning the information architecture and applying user centered design principles.

Our future goal is to continue growing our tool and visualizing more data. We know that we can help researchers to come to new findings in the medical field.
